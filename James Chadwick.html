James Chadwick

British scientist James Chadwick (1891-1974), Rutherford's student, demonstrated that
the previously unaccounted mass was due to neutrons, neutral particles in the nucleus.
The mass of a neutron is similar to that of a proton, but a neutron has no electrical
charge. In 1932, James Chadwick proposed:

He conducted an experiment where he shot alpha particles at Beryllium. The results show
high amounts of radiation that went through the Beryllium shield. The discovered particles
could not explain these results at the time. Chadwick was not convinced that these alpha
particles combined with only protons (at the time) could produce such a powerful energy
source. He figured out that there were particles without charge but that had a mass similar
to that of a proton.

  • Every atom is an empty sphere with a tiny dense nucleus at its centre.
  • Protons, which are positively charged, and neutrons, which are neutral particles, make up that nucleus.
  • The mass of a neutron is similar to that of a proton.
  • Negatively charged electrons rapidly orbit the nucleus through empty space.
  • The charge of the proton and the electron are equal in magnitude but opposite in sign.
