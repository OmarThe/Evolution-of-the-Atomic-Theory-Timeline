Erwin Schrödinger

In 1926, Erwin Schrödinger calculated the probability of detecting an electron in a specific
position using mathematical formulas. This quantum mechanical model does not define an
electron by the path it takes to orbit the nucleus; instead, it predicts the probability
of the electron being in any given place. A nucleus surrounded by an electron cloud can be
characterized as this model. The possibility of discovering an electron is higher in the
cloud's denser parts. In contrast, identifying an electron is more difficult in the cloud's
less dense regions.
