In 1927, Heisenberg's uncertainty principle stated that the more accurately you know the
position of an electron, the less accurately you can understand its velocity and vice versa.
The complementarity of the electron's wave nature and particle nature results in the
complementarity of velocity and position. This principle is only applicable at the atomic
level. To locate an electron inside an atom, a specialized microscope would be used to observe
its interaction with photons of light. Because a photon and an electron have comparable energy
levels, any attempt to locate an electron by interacting with a photon would result in the
electron being thrown off course of its orbit.
