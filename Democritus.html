Democritus

Around 400 B.C.E., Democritus (460-370 B.C.E.) and his mentor Leucippus were the first to propose
that matter was made up of minute, indestructible particles. The Greek philosopher Aristotle
argued that matter is made up of microscopic, indivisible components known as "atomos."
"Nothing exists except atoms and empty space; everything else is opinion.", Democritus wrote.
Democritus suggested that there were many various types of atoms, each with its own shape and size,
that travelled randomly through space.
