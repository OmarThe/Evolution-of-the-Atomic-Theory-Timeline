Dmitri Mendeleev

Dmitri Mendeleev (1834-1907), a Russian chemistry professor, saw a pattern in the properties
of elements. In 1869, Mendeleev's insight led to the periodic table, arguably the single most
crucial tool for the chemist. Dmitri created the periodic table by arranging the chemical
elements of his day in order of their atomic weights. He made new lines and categorized them
according to known behaviour patterns, allowing him to forecast the existence of three elements.
Using his periodic table's architecture, Mendeleev anticipated the properties of gallium, scandium,
and germanium. Let's think of Mendeleev's periodic table as a compact way to summarize many observations.
